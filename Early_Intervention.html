Early Intervention Programme


Early Intervention Program:



Rural Development Trust (RDT) has instituted a transformative Early Intervention program that operates at both the village and hospital levels, specifically designed for children below three years of age. The primary objective of this initiative is to proactively address the risk factors associated with the development of disabilities in young children and, where applicable, mitigate the severity of existing ones. This comprehensive facility extends its reach to cover children within the age group of 1-15 years in every village, emphasizing a holistic and inclusive approach to early childhood development.

At its core, the Early Intervention program aims to identify and address potential developmental challenges in children during their formative years. By intervening at an early stage, the program seeks to diminish the risk of disabilities taking root or alleviate the severity of pre-existing conditions. This strategic and proactive approach aligns with RDT's commitment to fostering the well-being of children from the outset, ensuring that they have every opportunity to lead healthy and fulfilling lives. The outreach of this program spans across villages, recognizing the importance of community-level engagement in nurturing the health and development of young minds. By integrating intervention measures within the fabric of rural life, RDT ensures that children, regardless of their geographical location, have access to vital early developmental support.

Established with a forward-looking perspective, the Early Intervention program operates on the premise that addressing developmental concerns in the early years can have a transformative impact on a child's future. By focusing on the 1-15 years age group, the initiative acknowledges the critical window for intervention during which the human brain is highly adaptable and responsive to positive influences. The overarching goal of the program is to not only mitigate immediate challenges but to pave the way for a trajectory toward healthier and more capable adulthood. RDT envisions a future where children who benefited from early intervention grow into resilient and self-sufficient adults, equipped to navigate life with confidence and independence.

Through the Early Intervention program, RDT bridges the gap between medical facilities and grassroots communities, ensuring that children receive timely and tailored support. By integrating these services into the fabric of village life, the program reinforces the notion that the health and development of children are integral components of community well-being.

Key Points:


  • This program can be available only for 1-15 years age group children
  • A doctor who is specialized particularly in the physiotherapy will make a visit to the particular village on the weekly basis during which he will treat the children accordingly with no cost of charge.
  • The doctor will give the exercises to be practiced to the children for his own transformation.
  • On the other hand, the parents are also being trained by the doctor to make the exercise done at the home.
  • In detail, these exercises help the children to transform their body from very young age.
