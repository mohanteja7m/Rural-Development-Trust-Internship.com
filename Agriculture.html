Agriculture and Husbandary



These activities are implemented to mitigate the effects of drought on the livelihood of the people and expand their sources of income, hence improving farmer's resilience.
  • Awareness programmes to promote alternative cropping patterns with crop choices that improve yields and are more profitable.
  • Promoting horticulture to improve area productivity per annum, decrease dependency on rains and provide long-term gains to the farmer.
  • Vermicomposting units and backyard farming to further the tenets of organic farming.
  • Dairy program that provides an assured income and reduces dependence on agriculture.
  • Promoting animal rearing such as piggery, poultry and pisciculture which also provides alternative livelihood as well as nutrition.

    Within the framework of the comprehensive Ecology Project, Rural Development Trust (RDT) undertakes a multifaceted approach to empower households and communities. One pivotal facet of this initiative involves the provision of livestock, including cows, buffaloes, or goats, to specific households with the overarching goal of enhancing income generation. This strategic intervention not only augments the financial resources of the households involved but also integrates sustainable practices into the fabric of rural life.

    The allocation of cattle, whether in the form of cows, buffaloes, or goats, represents a deliberate effort to uplift the economic standing of targeted households. By endowing families with these valuable assets, RDT seeks to create a sustainable avenue for income generation. The intrinsic value of such livestock lies not merely in their physical presence but in the myriad opportunities they present, particularly in the case of cows. Cows, as a cornerstone of this initiative, offer a dual benefit to the households. Primarily, they serve as a reliable source of milk production. The yield of milk from these cows becomes a valuable commodity that can be sold in local markets, thereby contributing to the financial well-being of the household. The economic impact is twofoldâ€”directly from the sale of milk and indirectly through the potential for additional dairy products that can be derived and sold.

    The process of selling milk emerges as a sustainable income stream for the households, allowing them to capitalize on the demand for dairy products within their communities. This not only provides a consistent source of revenue but also fosters economic independence, as families are no longer solely reliant on traditional income sources. Moreover, the introduction of cattle into the households aligns with the broader ecological objectives of the project. Livestock contribute to the sustainability of agricultural practices through natural fertilization, enhancing soil fertility and promoting environmentally friendly farming methods. The synergy between animal husbandry and agriculture not only benefits the individual households but also contributes to the overall health and resilience of the local ecosystem.

    The deliberate choice of cows, buffaloes, or goats as the focal points of this initiative reflects an understanding of the diverse needs and capacities of different households. While cows offer a consistent source of milk, buffaloes may provide higher milk yields, and goats may be well-suited for regions with specific environmental considerations. RDT's tailored approach underscores a commitment to sustainable and context-specific solutions that address the unique challenges faced by each household. In essence, the provision of cattle within the Ecology Project represents a transformative step toward holistic rural development. It goes beyond the conventional model of aid by empowering households with tangible assets that have the potential to perpetually uplift their economic status. As these animals become integral members of the community, the benefits extend beyond individual households, fostering a resilient and ecologically conscious rural landscape. Through this innovative initiative, RDT not only invests in the prosperity of households but also sows the seeds of sustainable development, ensuring a bountiful harvest for generations to come.

    Backyard Horticulture





    As an integral component of its commitment to improving nutrition at the grassroots level, Rural Development Trust (RDT) implements a visionary initiative by allocating backyards to households within the village for the purpose of fruit cultivation. This strategic intervention aims to bolster the nutritional intake of the local population, fostering a self-sustaining model where each household contributes to and benefits from the cultivation of fruits. By designating specific areas within households for fruit cultivation, RDT seeks to create decentralized sources of nutrition that are readily accessible to every family in the village. This initiative operates on the premise that a well-nourished community is a resilient and thriving one. The cultivation of fruits in individual backyards not only enhances the nutritional diversity within each household but also ensures an equitable distribution of these vital resources across the entire village.

    The fruits cultivated in these backyards serve as a direct and immediate source of nutrition for the households involved. Unlike other agricultural models focused on commercial sale, the primary consumption of these fruits is intended to be within the household itself. This approach not only addresses immediate nutritional needs but also instills a culture of self-sufficiency, where families are active contributors to their own well-being. The emphasis on equal distribution of nutrition is a cornerstone of this initiative. By empowering each household to cultivate fruits, RDT aims to eliminate disparities in nutritional access within the village. This egalitarian approach promotes community cohesion and ensures that every family benefits from the bounty of locally grown, nutrient-rich fruits. In doing so, the initiative goes beyond merely providing sustenance; it establishes a foundation for a healthier and more equitable community.

    Furthermore, the cultivation of fruits in household backyards aligns with the principles of sustainable agriculture. This decentralized model minimizes the environmental impact associated with large-scale farming and reduces the carbon footprint incurred in transporting produce from distant locations. The integration of fruit cultivation into the fabric of the village not only enhances nutrition but also contributes to the overall ecological sustainability of the community. In conclusion, RDT's provision of backyards for fruit cultivation emerges as a transformative approach to addressing nutrition at the grassroots level. By empowering households to cultivate fruits, the initiative not only enriches individual diets but also establishes a collective framework for community well-being. Through this decentralized and equitable model, RDT endeavors to create a resilient village where the fruits of nutrition are shared by all, fostering a sustainable and nourished community for generations to come.

    Poultry




    In the pursuit of economic upliftment, RDT recognizes the potential of poultry farming, specifically through the distribution of hens to individual households. The primary focus is on leveraging the prolific egg-laying capabilities of hens as a means of income generation. Each hen has the capacity to yield approximately 100 to 150 eggs over a given period, providing families with a consistent and renewable source of revenue through the sale of these eggs.

    The economic impact of this initiative extends beyond the immediate sale of eggs. It creates a sustainable and scalable income stream for households, empowering them with financial stability. By engaging in poultry farming, families are afforded the opportunity to diversify their income sources, reducing dependency on traditional forms of livelihood. This diversification not only strengthens the economic resilience of individual households but also contributes to the overall economic vibrancy of the village. Simultaneously, the provision of hens contributes significantly to addressing nutritional deficiencies within the community. Eggs, being a rich source of protein, vitamins, and minerals, become an essential component of a balanced diet. The inclusion of eggs in the household's daily meals enhances the overall nutritional intake, especially in regions where access to diverse and nutrient-rich food is limited.

    Beyond the economic and nutritional benefits, the poultry initiative promotes a sense of ownership and responsibility among households. Caring for the hens requires attention and diligence, instilling a sense of pride and engagement within the community. As families actively participate in poultry farming, they develop valuable skills in animal husbandry, contributing to their agricultural knowledge base and fostering a culture of self-reliance. Furthermore, the ripple effects of this initiative extend to the broader community. As households engage in egg production, there emerges a potential surplus that can be channeled back into the local economy. The sale of eggs in nearby markets not only generates additional income for families but also contributes to the availability of affordable and locally sourced protein for the wider community.

    Welfare Schemes




    In a concerted effort to modernize agricultural practices and empower local farmers, Rural Development Trust (RDT) has introduced a valuable arsenal of agricultural machinery in the village, comprising five power weeders and two power tillers. This initiative is designed to revolutionize traditional farming methods, making the process more efficient and enhancing the productivity of agricultural endeavors. The deployment of power weeders and power tillers not only facilitates the plantation of crops but also represents a commitment to equipping agricultural workers with the necessary skills through comprehensive training programs.

    The five power weeders and two power tillers constitute a technological boon for the village, signaling a departure from labor-intensive practices towards a more mechanized and streamlined approach to agriculture. Power weeders, in particular, play a pivotal role in the precise plantation of crops in the fields. These machines are adept at creating well-defined lines for each crop, optimizing spacing and facilitating efficient planting, thereby contributing to improved crop yields. Recognizing the transformative potential of these agricultural machines, RDT goes beyond merely providing the equipment. The organization places a strong emphasis on ensuring that farmers are equipped with the knowledge and skills required to operate these machines safely and effectively. Through dedicated training programs, agricultural workers in the village receive comprehensive instruction on the proper handling and maintenance of power weeders and power tillers.

    RDT's provision of five power weeders and two power tillers represents a holistic approach to advancing agricultural practices in the village. Through the deployment of modern machinery and comprehensive training programs, the organization aims to empower local farmers, ushering in a new era of efficiency, safety, and economic prosperity. By merging technological innovation with skill development, RDT not only transforms the landscape of agriculture in the village but also empowers the agricultural workforce to navigate the challenges of the future with confidence and proficiency.
